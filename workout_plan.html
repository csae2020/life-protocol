This program is built on five key principles to maximize your results. Understanding these will help you stay consistent and make smart adjustments as you progress.</p>

                        <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-700">1. Progressive Overload</h3>

                        <p class="text-stone-700">This is the most important principle. To get bigger and stronger, you must continually make your muscles work harder than they're used to. Since you preferred adding weight, the plan is simple:</p>

                        <p class="font-semibold text-sky-800 bg-sky-50 p-3 rounded-md">Your Rule: When you successfully complete all sets and reps for an exercise at a given weight, add the smallest available weight (e.g., 1.25kg / 2.5lbs per side) the *next* time you do that exercise.</p>


                        <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-700">2. Frequency (2x/Week)</h3>

                        <p class="text-stone-700">Research shows that hitting a muscle group twice per week (like in this PPL split) is superior for muscle growth compared to a traditional "bro split" (once per week). This keeps muscle protein synthesis elevated more often.</p>


                        <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-700">3. Volume (Total Work)</h3>

                        <p class="text-stone-700">Volume (Sets x Reps x Weight) is a key driver of hypertrophy. This plan includes high volume, especially for your priority areas (Legs, Chest, Arms). The chart shows the approximate weekly set volume distribution for this plan, reflecting your goals.</p>

                        <div class="chart-container">

                            <canvas id="volumeChart"></canvas>

                        </div>

                    </div>

                    <div class="bg-white p-6 rounded-lg shadow border border-stone-200 space-y-4">

                        <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-700">4. Intensity (%1RM & Rep Ranges)</h3>

                        <p class="text-stone-700">We use different rep ranges to target both strength and hypertrophy:</p>

                        <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-2 text-stone-700">

                            <li><strong class="text-stone-800">Strength (4-6 Reps):</strong> Uses heavy loads (80-85%+ 1RM) to increase neural drive and maximal force. This is for your Bench Press 'A' day.</li>

                            <li><strong class="text-stone-800">Hypertrophy (8-12 Reps):</strong> The "sweet spot" for muscle growth, balancing mechanical tension and metabolic stress.</li>

                            <li><strong class="text-stone-800">Metabolic Stress (12-20+ Reps):</strong> Creates a "pump" and forces muscle adaptation through different pathways. Used for isolation moves (forearms, calves, leg extensions).</li>

                        </ul>


                        <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-sky-700">5. Rest Periods</h3>

                        <p class="text-stone-700">Since effectiveness is your goal (not efficiency), rest periods are set for full recovery. This allows you to lift heavier on each set, leading to better progressive overload.</p>

                        <ul class="list-disc list-inside space-y-2 text-stone-700">

                            <li><strong class="text-stone-800">3-5 Minutes:</strong> For heavy compound lifts (Bench, Squat, Deadlift).</li>

                            <li><strong class="text-stone-800">90 Secs - 2.5 Mins:</strong> For accessory/hypertrophy lifts (Rows, Curls, Extensions).</li>

                        </ul>

                const weight = parseFloat(calcWeight.value);

                const reps = parseInt(calcReps.value);


                if (!weight || !reps || weight <= 0 || reps <= 0) {

                    alert('Please enter valid numbers for weight and reps.');

                    return;

                }

                

                if (reps === 1) {

                    oneRepMax = weight;

                } else if (reps > 12) {

                    alert('Please enter reps between 1 and 12 for the most accurate estimate.');

                    return;

                } else {

                    oneRepMax = weight / (1.0278 - (0.0278 * reps));

                }


                calcResult.textContent = oneRepMax.toFixed(1);

                calcResultContainer.classList.remove('hidden');


                updateProgressionTable(oneRepMax);

            });
