Overview

This hybrid upper/lower protocol blends strength-specific sessions with higher-volume hypertrophy work. The layout provides quick-glance coaching cues alongside complete session breakdowns so you can stay focused on intent.

Weekly Rhythm

  • 6 training days across Upper A/B/C and Lower A/B/C.
  • 2× frequency per muscle group to keep muscle protein synthesis elevated.
  • Embedded linear load progressions with scheduled deloads on Weeks 5 and 10.

Key Metrics

  • Track hard sets per muscle (10–20+ weekly).
  • Anchor effort with reps in reserve (RIR): 0–1 on final sets of priority lifts.
  • Use the 1RM calculator to set starting loads without max testing.

The Framework for Muscle Adaptation

Separate stimuli drive neural strength and muscular size. This plan places both under one umbrella by alternating heavy sessions with metabolically demanding volume work.

Strength vs. Hypertrophy

  • Strength: heavy loads (>80% 1RM), low reps (1–5), long rest (3–5 min).
  • Hypertrophy: moderate loads (60–85% 1RM), 6–20 reps, rest 60–180 sec.
  • Neural efficiency and myofibrillar growth for strength; sarcoplasmic expansion adds size.

Two Hypertrophy Pathways

  • Myofibrillar: Dense contractile tissue from heavy pressing, squatting, and rowing.
  • Sarcoplasmic: Higher-rep metabolic stress from flyes, curls, extensions, machines.
  • Alternating emphasis ensures larger muscle cross-sectional area and higher force potential.

Programming Variables

Manipulate these levers intentionally—volume, intensity, rest, and frequency—to signal the adaptation you need on any given day.

Training Volume

Primary driver of hypertrophy. Target 10–20+ hard sets per muscle group each week, adjusting based on recovery and rate of progress.

Intensity & Repetition Zones

Goal Primary Adaptation Intensity (%1RM) Repetition Range Inter-set Rest
Strength Neuromuscular + Myofibrillar >80% 1–5 3–5 min
Hypertrophy Myofibrillar + Sarcoplasmic 60–85% 6–20+ 1–3 min

Rest Periods

  • Heavy compounds: 3–5 minutes to fully replenish ATP/PC stores.
  • Moderate compounds: 120–180 seconds to balance output and metabolic stress.
  • Isolation work: 60–90 seconds; shorten slightly during pump finishers.

Training Frequency

Splitting volume across two weekly touches per muscle group improves set quality, total tonnage, and skill practice on key lifts.

Linear Progression & Overload

Progressive overload remains the non-negotiable. Linearly increase stress, adjust when reps stall, and rotate overload methods when necessary.

Primary Method

  • Establish baseline loads from the calculator.
  • Add weight weekly: Upper body +1.25–2.5 kg, Lower body +2.5–5 kg.
  • If you miss the prescription, repeat the same weight next session before progressing.

Alternative Overload

  • Extra repetitions at the same load.
  • Additional hard sets (while monitoring fatigue).
  • Improved range of motion or tighter tempo.
  • Reduced rest intervals (for hypertrophy days only).
Plateau Check: If lifts stall for two consecutive weeks, assess sleep, nutrition, and stress. When those are on point, consider a technique refinement block, micro-loading, or an extra rest day.

Recovery Architecture

Training stress drives adaptation only when recovery completes the cycle. Build nutrition, sleep, and strategic deloads into the plan.

Daily Recovery

  • Sleep: 7–9 hours; anchor bedtime and wake-up times.
  • Protein: 1.6–2.2 g/kg bodyweight per day split across 4–5 meals.
  • Hydration: ~35–40 ml/kg, plus electrolytes during longer sessions.

Deload Protocol

  • Every Week 5 & 10 reduce volume by ~40%.
  • Lower intensity to 50–60% of usual working loads.
  • Focus on technique, mobility, and sleep—supercompensation follows.

A single deload week preserves strength and muscle while clearing fatigue. Treat it as active recovery, not lost progress.

Execution & Rationale Notes

Use these cues to keep technique sharp and ensure each exercise hits its intended muscle group or sticking point.

Chest & Pressing Performance

  • Barbell Bench Press: retract scapulae, slight arch, pause on chest for raw strength.
  • Incline Press: 15–30° bench. Drive dumbbells slightly inward to bias upper fibers.
  • Weighted Dips: maintain forward lean to recruit lower chest while respecting shoulder comfort.

Bench Press Assistance

  • Close-Grip Bench boosts lockout power via triceps overload.
  • Paused Bench Variations eliminate stretch reflex, reinforcing strength off the chest.
  • Rows & Face Pulls create a stable pressing platform and shoulder health.

Lower Body Development

  • Back Squat: full-depth squats double quad hypertrophy versus partials.
  • Romanian Deadlift: hinge from hips, maintain hamstring tension throughout.
  • Machine Work: leg press, hack squat, and extensions provide safe failure-ready volume.

Arm & Forearm Focus

  • Incline Curls: shoulder extension stretches the long head for peak shape.
  • Overhead Extensions: lengthened triceps long head equals maximal recruitment.
  • Hammer & Wrist Variations: round out brachialis and forearm development for overall arm density.
